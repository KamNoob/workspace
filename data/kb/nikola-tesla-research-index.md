## Major Projects (Verified)

### ✅ COMPLETED & COMMERCIALIZED

1. **Alternating Current (AC) System** (1883-1895)
   - Modern electrical power distribution
   - Patents: US390415 + 7 polyphase patents (1888)
   - Status: Global standard today
   - Document: "The Inventions, Researches and Writings..." (1894)

2. **Induction Motor with Rotating Magnetic Field** (1883-1888)
   - First practical AC motor
   - 7 U.S. patents (1888)
   - Status: Foundation of modern machinery

3. **Tesla Coil Transformer** (1891-1897)
   - Electrical transformer for high voltage/frequency
   - Patents: US568176A, US568179A (1896)
   - 20,000 cycles/second at extreme voltages
   - Status: Still used in radio & wireless tech

4. **Remote Control Technology** (1898)
   - Teleautomatic boat - radio-guided vessel
   - Demonstrated: Madison Square Garden, NYC
   - Status: Early wireless control pioneer

5. **Neon & Phosphorescent Lighting** (1890s-1900s)
   - High-frequency power → neon light creation
   - Status: Foundation of modern neon signage

### 🔬 EXPERIMENTAL & DOCUMENTED

6. **Wireless Power Transmission System** (1891-1905)
   - Long-distance power without wires
   - Patents: US649621A (1900)
   - Wardenclyffe Tower (Long Island) - partially built
   - Status: Theoretical success; never scaled to production

7. **Colorado Springs Laboratory** (1899-1900)
   - High-voltage & high-frequency experiments
   - 60+ technical records & findings
   - Status: Comprehensive documentation exists

8. **X-Ray Research / Shadowgraphy** (1891-1895)
   - Parallel work to Röntgen's X-ray discovery
   - Status: Experimental evidence documented

9. **Tesla Turbine (Bladeless Rotor)** (1906-1913)
   - Turbine design using rotating disk
   - Patents: Multiple turbine patents
   - Status: Design documented; prototypes built

10. **Mechanical Oscillator** (1890s-1912)
    - "Earthquake Machine" - resonance-based vibrator
    - Status: Prototype documented; some claims legendary

### ⚠️ THEORETICAL/UNVERIFIED

11. **Particle Beam Weapon (Teleforce)** (1930s-1942)
    - "Death Ray" / "Peace Beam"
    - 48x speed of sound particle acceleration
    - Offered to: US, USSR (USSR tested 1939)
    - Status: Theoretical; CIA documents declassified

12. **Thought Photography Device** (1920s-1930s)
    - Claimed to project thoughts as screen images
    - Status: No experimental evidence; theoretical

13. **Cosmic Ray Motor** (Later years)
    - Motor powered by cosmic radiation
    - Status: Theoretical concept only

## Archived Documents

### PRIMARY ARCHIVES

#### 1. Nikola Tesla Museum (Belgrade, Serbia)
- **Holdings:** Personal papers, notebooks, correspondence, designs, photographs
- **When Acquired:** 1952
- **Access:** In-person + Academia.edu digital
- **URL:** https://tesla-museum.org/

#### 2. Library of Congress (Washington, D.C.)
- **Collection:** Tesla Correspondence 1890-1934
- **Format:** Microfilm edition
- **Key Correspondents:** Mark Twain, Robert Underwood Johnson, J.P. Morgan, George Westinghouse
- **Access:** Manuscript Division (by appointment)
- **URL:** https://www.loc.gov/item/mm82050302/

#### 3. Smithsonian Institution (Washington, D.C.)
- **Collection:** H.W. Lende Jr. Manuscript Collection
- **Content:** Wireless power transmission designs, technical documents
- **Access:** Library systems
- **URL:** https://library.si.edu/

#### 4. Internet Archive (Free Online)
- **Collection:** Complete Tesla Papers
- **Books Included:** Inventions, Researches and Writings (1894)
- **Access:** Free download/streaming
- **URL:** https://archive.org/details/nikolateslapape00tesl

### PUBLISHED COMPILATIONS

1. **My Inventions: The Autobiography of Nikola Tesla**
   - Compiled by: Ben Johnston (1983)
   - Original: 1919 magazine series (republished 1977)
   - Content: Tesla's own account

2. **The Fantastic Inventions of Nikola Tesla**
   - Compiler: David Hatcher Childress (1993)
   - Content: Collection of articles

3. **The Inventions, Researches and Writings of Nikola Tesla**
   - Editor: Thomas Commerford Martin (1894)
   - Focus: Polyphase currents & high-potential lighting
   - Access: Free (Internet Archive)

4. **Tesla Said: Collected Articles and Statements**
   - Compiler: John T. Ratzlaff
   - Sources: Electrical World, Scientific American, Science News
   - Access: Academic libraries

### KEY PUBLISHED ARTICLES

- **"The Problem of Increasing Human Energy"** (The Century Magazine, 1900)
- **"Experiments with Alternate Currents of High Potential and High Frequency"** (1894 book)
- Both available free online
